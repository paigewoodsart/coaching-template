About

A different kind of coaching.

For the past several years, I have worked across branding, media consulting, creative direction, nonprofit strategy, and personal transformation work.

What started as graphic design and branding slowly evolved into something much deeper.

I noticed that most people were not struggling because they lacked talent or ambition. They were struggling because they were disconnected from themselves: disconnected from rest, from joy, from creativity, from their own intuition.

So many people are trying to build lives that look successful while quietly feeling exhausted, emotionally disconnected, or trapped inside systems that no longer fit them.

My work exists to help people return to themselves. I work especially well with creatives navigating burnout, freelancers building sustainable lives, and people who feel stuck between practicality and meaning.

My coaching style is thoughtful, grounded, collaborative, and deeply human. You do not need to simplify yourself to do meaningful growth work.

Aligned living is not perfection.

Sustainable growth over constant hustle

Emotional honesty over performance

Curiosity over shame

Structure that supports freedom

Creative expression as a form of healing

Building a life that feels internally coherent

Sometimes it looks like finally admitting you want something different.

Process

What working together looks like.

01

Reflection

We begin by understanding where you are right now: emotionally, creatively, practically, and relationally. Not just what you do, but how your life actually feels.

02

Clarity

We identify the patterns, pressures, and beliefs creating disconnection or burnout, then reconnect with your real values, desires, rhythms, and needs.

03

Alignment

Together we build structures, goals, routines, and decisions that support the life you genuinely want to live, not a version built around outside expectations.

04

Integration

Real change happens when insight becomes part of daily life. This phase focuses on sustainability and helping change feel realistic rather than overwhelming.
